Client Intelligence lets you capture context about a client beyond what automated research finds. Sales notes, meeting takeaways, competitive intel, and article summaries all feed into generation to produce more targeted content.

## Where to Find It

The Intelligence tab is on each client's hub page. Click into any client and select the **Intelligence** tab.

## Two Types of Intelligence

### Quick Notes

Free-form markdown notes about the client. Use these for:

- Meeting notes and takeaways
- Sales observations ("CFO is the real decision-maker")
- Competitive context ("They're evaluating Accenture for the same work")
- Internal strategy notes

Quick notes are saved as rich text on the client record.

### Structured Entries

Tagged intelligence items with metadata. Each entry has:

| Field          | Description                                                            |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Type**       | `news`, `earnings`, `strategy`, `competitive`, `technology`, `culture` |
| **Title**      | Short description of the intelligence                                  |
| **Content**    | The actual insight or summary                                          |
| **Source URL** | Where this came from (optional)                                        |
| **Date**       | When this was captured                                                 |

#### URL Summarization

Paste a URL into the source field and click **Summarize** - the system fetches the page and uses AI to generate a structured summary. This is useful for quickly capturing:

- News articles about the company
- Blog posts or press releases
- Earnings call transcripts
- Industry analyst reports

The AI extracts the key points and creates a structured entry automatically.

## How Intelligence Feeds Generation

When you generate ideas, sales pages, partner pages, or briefs, the system:

1. Loads all intelligence entries and notes for that client
2. Formats them as additional context appended to the source of truth
3. Claude uses this context alongside research to produce more targeted content

More intelligence = better output. A client with detailed notes and 5-10 structured entries will get noticeably more specific, relevant pitches than one with research alone.

<Aside type="tip">
  Add intelligence **before** generating content. The best time is right after research - review the
  source of truth, then add anything the automated research missed. Meeting notes, internal
  observations, and competitive intel make the biggest difference.
</Aside>
