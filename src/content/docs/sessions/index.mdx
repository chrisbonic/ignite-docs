Every session ends with a `wrap-up` ritual that produces a **shipped manifest** — a technical record of what landed in the repo, what didn't ship, and how a future session should pick up.

## How manifests are authored

The `wrap-up` skill (`.claude/skills/wrap-up/`) writes to `sessions/session-{N}-{topic}.mdx` at session end. Each manifest includes:

- **Commit SHAs + date** at the top
- **What shipped** — concrete changes (files, features, behavior)
- **Not yet validated** — built but not QA'd live
- **Known deferred** — what the session did not ship, with reasons
- **How to apply** — one line for future-Claude ("next session should X")

These are the **canonical record** of repo evolution. More durable than commit messages, more structured than session-start notes.
