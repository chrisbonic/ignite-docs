These rules apply to all generated and edited content across the platform.

## The Core Message

> **Your On-Demand Innovation Team. Results Included.**

Ignite Studio is an on-demand innovation acceleration team for enterprise prospects. The team is deployed to solve a specific challenge, build a prototype, and hand off the win.

## Always

- **Ignite leads** - partners are "brought to the table" to complement Ignite's capabilities
- Use **platform/operator language**: deploy, integrate, build, measure, ship
- **Step 4 is "Your Team Presents"** - the prospect's team takes the credit
- Keep headlines tight - 3 lines max, ~20 characters per line
- Use **regular dashes** for punctuation
- Source all executive quotes with verifiable citations
- Use the **dark theme** brand system consistently

## Never

| Rule                                        | Why                                           |
| ------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Never say **"Powered by"**                  | Minimizes Ignite's role - we lead, not power  |
| Never show **idea counts** ("8 ideas")      | The portfolio is always evolving              |
| Never use **em dashes**                     | Brand style uses regular dashes               |
| Never use **fake numbers**                  | If you can't defend it, don't include it      |
| Never include **SOWs, timelines, or costs** | Pitches are about possibility, not commitment |
| Never include **total idea counts**         | "Idea 3" is fine, "Idea 3 of 8" is not        |

<Aside type="caution">
  The "powered by" rule is especially important for partner pages. Even when showcasing a partner,
  Ignite is the team that delivers. Partners are capabilities we bring to the engagement.
</Aside>

## Tone and Voice

- **Confident** but not arrogant
- **Specific** to their business (use their vocabulary, their challenges)
- **Forward-looking** - focus on what's possible, not what's broken
- **Concise** - every word earns its place
- **Provocative** in headlines - make them stop scrolling

## Content Quality Standards

### Executive Quotes

- Must be **real and verifiable**
- Source cited (earnings call transcript, press release, published interview)
- Never fabricated, even if it "sounds right"
- If no exec quote exists, use an industry analyst quote with source

### Scenarios (Slide 5)

- Must be **conceptual**, not specific
- No fake metrics ("save 40% on costs") unless backed by cited research
- Pick the two most **distinct** scenarios - don't repeat the same idea twice

### Visual Diagrams (Slide 4)

- Follow the **Input -> Engine -> Output** pattern
- The diagram is the **star** - text supports it, not the other way around
- Use glass panels and brand colors
- Test that it renders correctly before sharing
