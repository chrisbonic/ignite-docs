Every idea pitch page follows this exact structure. Each "slide" is a full-width section. The entire page reads in approximately 60 seconds.

## Slide 1: The Big Idea (Hero)

The hero is a full-viewport section with a brand image background. It presents one bold competitive concept - not a product description.

**Requirements:**

- **MAX 3 lines in the h1**, ~20 characters per line at 5rem uppercase Urbanist
- Longer lines wrap on desktop and create 4-5 visual lines - tighter is always better
- One-sentence subtitle reinforcing urgency
- Idea counter: "Idea XX" format (no totals - the portfolio is always evolving)
- Brand hero image auto-assigned via `pickBrandHeroImage()`

<Aside type="tip">
  The hero headline is the most important piece of copy on the page. It should be a provocation, not
  a description. Think "Kill the Bottleneck" not "Supply Chain Optimization Tool."
</Aside>

## Slide 2: The Executive Quote

A real, verified quote from the prospect's leadership. This creates instant credibility - the prospect sees their own words reflected back.

**Requirements:**

- **Must be a real, verified quote** from earnings calls, press releases, or interviews
- Include the source in the attribution (e.g., "Q4 2025 Earnings Call")
- Key phrase highlighted in indigo
- Label: "From Your Leadership" / "The Signal" / "From Your CFO" or similar
- **Never fabricate or paraphrase** a quote attributed to a named executive

If no executive quote exists, use an industry analyst quote or market data point with a source.

## Slide 3: The Gap

The problem section - framed in THEIR vocabulary, not yours. The prospect should nod before you pitch.

**Requirements:**

- Headline passes the "that's me" test
- 3 glass cards naming symptoms (parallel structure)
- No body paragraph under the headline - heading + cards say enough
- No solving yet. Just pain.

## Slide 4: The Idea

What this actually is. The visual diagram is the star of this section.

**Requirements:**

- One heading + one line of description
- A **visual diagram** that makes the concept instantly legible (flow diagram, before/after, architecture)
- The visual follows an **Input -> Engine -> Output** flow pattern
- Text supports the visual, not the other way around

## Slide 5: Imagine This

Two scenarios making the idea tangible.

**Requirements:**

- Exactly **2 scenarios** (not 4, not 1)
- No fake numbers or undefensible stats
- Conceptual enough to be credible
- Pick the two **most distinct** scenarios

## Slide 6: Delivery Approach

How Ignite delivers - without commitment.

**Requirements:**

- **4 steps**, no timelines, costs, or commitments
- Step 4 is **ALWAYS** "Your Team Presents" - this is non-negotiable
- Heading: "Prototype to Proof. Your Team Presents the Win."

<Aside type="caution">
  Step 4 ("Your Team Presents") is hardcoded in the component. Don't try to change it in the CMS -
  the component renders it automatically.
</Aside>

## Slide 7: CTA

The close.

**Requirements:**

- Heading: "Ready to Build Something Together?"
- Sub: "We'll bring the team, the tools, and the proof and work with your existing stack and staff."
- **"Book a Call"** (primary button) + **"View More Opportunities"** (ghost link back to package)
